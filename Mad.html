MAD

A Roman Bacchanal to celebrate the opening of 'Bvlgari & Rome: Eternal Inspiration' exhibition: An indulgent banquet for 50

An evening based on the Ancient Roman festivals of the cult of Bacchus, “god of wine, freedom, and ecstasy.” Bacchanalia are held in strict privacy, and initiates are bound to secrecy.

Proposed triclinium seating arrangement: three tables in a U shape in the triclinium (formal dining room.) Revelers recline to eat, drink, and converse.

Floral design compliments the fresco palate, and is inspired by the richly detailed frescoes of the painted garden at Villa of Liva in Prima Porta: Fruiting pomegranites and citrus trees mingle with lush laurel, ivy, Italian Cyprus, date palms, as well as flowering acanthus, rose, poppy, chrysanthemum, chamomile violet, and iris Décor color palette is based on the deep, earthy colors of Roman frescoes: Pompey Red, Burnt Sienna. Etched roman glass for tableware.
